Qualification
  • Requires technical knowledge in the field acquired through an apprenticeship, a 2-year degree, or professional certificate
  • B.S. in Wildlife Ecology or a related discipline, M.S. in Wildlife Ecology or a related discipline, experience with processing auditory data, coding in R and Python, and willingness to obtain experience refining existing convolutional neural networks to improve efficiency of data processing
  • A degree in computer science or related discipline may suffice if the candidate has a keen interest in wildlife science
Responsibilities
  • The Research Associate will be responsible for directing the processing of acoustic recording data collected from acoustic recording units and images collected from mobile phones and trail cameras focused on estimating population abundance of northern bobwhite, gobbling chronology of wild turkeys, occupancy of grassland birds, identifying predator species, and identifying plant communities
  • The candidate will process and verify these data using convolutional neural networks and perhaps newer models (e.g., transformer models), and provide data summaries and analyses to agencies and landowners providing funding to the labs of Drs
  • Standard work in an office setting
